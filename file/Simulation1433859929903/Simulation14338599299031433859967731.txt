<h2>Welcome to iGEM</h2>
<p>The iGEM Foundation is dedicated to education and competition, advancement of synthetic biology, and the development of open community and collaboration.</p>
<p>The main program at the iGEM Foundation is the International Genetically Engineered Machine (iGEM) Competition.  The iGEM Competition is the premiere student competition in Synthetic Biology. Since 2004, participants of the competition have experienced education, teamwork, sharing, and more in a unique competition setting.</p>
<p>iGEM is also much more than a competition; our community has a long history of involving students and the public in the development of the new field of synthetic biology.</p>

<h3>iGEM Programs</h3>
<div class="highlights_MainPage">


<h4>Competition</h4>
<p> iGEM runs the premiere student competition in Synthetic Biology. Student teams are given a kit of biological parts and work over the summer to build biological systems and operate them in living cells. Read more about this year's competition at <a href="http://2015.igem.org">2015.igem.org</a>. Or, read more about previous years on the<a href=""> Previous Competitions </a>page.</p>
</p>
</div>

<div class="highlights_MainPage">
<h4>Academic Labs</h4>
<p>Not competing? Academic labs have a place in the iGEM community as well. Labs can subscribe to the program to conduct their own research using synthetic biology based on parts, and have access to the same resources as competition teams. <br><br>
Read more on the <a href="http://igem.org/Labs">Labs program </a>page.</p>
</div>


<div class="highlights_MainPage">
<h4>Registry</h4>
<p> iGEM Headquarters also operates and maintains the <a href="http://parts.igem.org">Registry of Standard Biological Parts</a>. The Registry has over 20,000 standard biological parts, with open access to all participating groups. Teams and labs contribute their parts in the spirit of the "Get &amp; Give" philosophy. <br><br>
Visit the Registry at <a href="http://parts.igem.org">parts.igem.org</a>.</p>
</div>
