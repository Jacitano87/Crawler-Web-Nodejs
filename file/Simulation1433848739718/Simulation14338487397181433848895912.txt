	<h2 class="headline small"><a href="http://isbmolecularme.com/new-open-access-multiscale-model-captures-dynamic-molecular-processes-in-unprecedented-detail/" rel="bookmark">New Open-Access Multiscale Model Captures Dynamic Molecular Processes in Unprecedented Detail</a></h2>
	
	<div class="post-author">
		<p class="align-left"><i class="icon-time"></i> &nbsp;Posted on July 15, 2014 by <a href="http://isbmolecularme.com/author/admin/" title="Posts by ISBUSA" rel="author">ISBUSA</a></p>
		<p class="align-right"><i class="icon-comment"></i> &nbsp;<a href="http://isbmolecularme.com/new-open-access-multiscale-model-captures-dynamic-molecular-processes-in-unprecedented-detail/#comments">Leave a Comment</a></p>

						<a class="feature-img" href="http://isbmolecularme.com/new-open-access-multiscale-model-captures-dynamic-molecular-processes-in-unprecedented-detail/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to New Open-Access Multiscale Model Captures Dynamic Molecular Processes in Unprecedented Detail"><img src="http://isbmolecularme.com/wp-content/uploads/egrin_MM.jpg" class="attachment-featured-large wp-post-image" alt="Decoding the Microbial Gene-Recycling Program: Researchers &#039;Unzip&#039; Genetic Instruction Manuals" /></a>
				
	<p>3 Bullets: Microbes are efficient because their streamlined genomes allow them to evolve and adapt rapidly to complex environmental changes. Decoding the highly-compressed information within a microbial genome requires sophisticated systems biology tools to map the genetic programs, and understand how they are executed&#8230;.</p>

Allison Lee"><img src="http://isbmolecularme.com/wp-content/uploads/PD5A2243-300x199.jpg" alt="Click to learn more about the Minds of ISB who infuse this place with a vibrant, collaborative, entrepreneurial and driven spirit.  Allison Lee" title="Click to learn more about the Minds of ISB who infuse this place with a vibrant, collaborative, entrepreneurial and driven spirit.  Allison Lee" width="300" height="199" class="rsg_image" /></a>

I spent three years in an immunology lab thinking that's what lab research scientists did. I hated it. I instead  became a field biologist and gallivanted around the woods collecting observational data on songbirds, squirrels, parrots, and now I work with algae and ocean acidification issues. I realized that the lab bench skills I had learned in the immunology lab could be applied to different research projects that were more in my field of interest and I was hooked. It wasn't lab research science I hated; I had just been in the wrong context!"  - ALLISON LEE, Research Associate 2, Baliga Lab</dd>
