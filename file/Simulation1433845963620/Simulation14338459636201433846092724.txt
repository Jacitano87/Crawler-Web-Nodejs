<p>The following Insight Papers are authored by 2015 Fellows or groups of Fellows, based on their experience during the 2015 Landscaping meetings. <div id="leap-technology-doesnt-sound-like-the-problem-2" class="noakes-accordion-item paper">
<div><a href="javascript:void(0);" class="noakes-accordion-item-button">&#8220;Technology Doesn&#8217;t Sound Like the Problem&#8221;<br /><span>Sanjana Ravi, Nicola Patron, Ted Fjällman, Karen Ingram</span></a></div>
<div class="noakes-accordion-item-content">
<p><strong>&#8220;Technology Doesn&#8217;t Sound Like the Problem&#8221;<br /><a href="http://synbioleap.org/community-member/sanjana-ravi/">Sanjana Ravi</a>, <a href="http://synbioleap.org/community-member/nicola-patron/">Nicola Patron</a>, <a href="http://synbioleap.org/community-member/ted-fjallman/">Ted Fjällman</a>, <a href="http://synbioleap.org/community-member/karen-ingram/">Karen Ingram</a></strong></p>
<p>&#8220;Technology doesn&#8217;t sound like the problem.&#8221; It might have been pre-guaranteed that someone would conclude this after hearing the achievements and future aims of the members this incredibly talented group, which we are humbled to be a part of. While there are still some significant technological leaps to be made, the main impediments to synthetic biology being able to make the world a better place were not thought to lie in the laboratory. Several themes emerged over the course of the workshop that promise to shape the way this year&#8217;s LEAP fellows consider challenges and opportunities in this space: the need for communication and coordination within the synthetic biology community, useful examples of success to ensure continued investment, effective regulation to mitigate the environmental and security risks of this rapidly advancing and increasingly open technology, and engaging the public to allay fears of new and emerging biotechnologies:</p>
<p><a href="http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/Technology-Doesnt-Sound-Like-the-Problem.pdf" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','download','http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/Technology-Doesnt-Sound-Like-the-Problem.pdf']);" target="_blank">Download PDF &raquo;</a></div>
</div>
<div id="leap-uk-academic-synbio-landscape" class="noakes-accordion-item paper">
<div><a href="javascript:void(0);" class="noakes-accordion-item-button">UK Academic SynBio Landscape<br /><span>Jon Marles-Wright and Sarah Richardson</span></a></div>
<div class="noakes-accordion-item-content">
<p><strong>UK Academic SynBio Landscape<br /><a href="http://synbioleap.org/community-member/jon-marles-wright/">Jon Marles-Wright</a> and <a href="http://synbioleap.org/community-member/sarah-richardson/">Sarah Richardson</a></strong></p>
<p>Looking at the potential applications and markets for synthetic biology from a UK academic perspective the <a href="http://www.rcuk.ac.uk/publications/reports/syntheticbiologyroadmap/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.rcuk.ac.uk/publications/reports/syntheticbiologyroadmap/', 'Synthetic Biology Roadmap']);" target="_blank">Synthetic Biology Roadmap</a> provides a clear overview of what the opportunities and challenges for the field will be for the next ten years. The Roadmap was conceived and produced by the UK government with stakeholder groups from academia, industry and policy groups. This document sets out a vision for the development of SynBio in the UK and identifies technologies and application areas where the field has the potential to make significant impacts. The authors also make a series of recommendations for investment in the field that has so far seen the a commitment of over £100 million to fund a network of SynBio research centres, an Innovation and Knowledge Transfer Hub, and initiatives to bring together academics and industrial interests through the <a href="http://www.bbsrc.ac.uk/ibnetworks" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bbsrc.ac.uk/ibnetworks', 'Networks in Industrial Biotechnology']);" target="_blank">Networks in Industrial Biotechnology</a>. Additional funds have been allocated to a network of ‘<a href="http://www.bbsrc.ac.uk/news/research-technologies/2014/140402-pr-synthetic-biology-facilities-training/" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.bbsrc.ac.uk/news/research-technologies/2014/140402-pr-synthetic-biology-facilities-training/', 'Genome Foundries']);" target="_blank">Genome Foundries</a>’ to develop technologies and pipelines for large-scale DNA assembly automation.</p>
<p><a href="http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/The-UK-Academic-SynBio-Landscape.pdf" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','download','http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/The-UK-Academic-SynBio-Landscape.pdf']);" target="_blank">Download PDF &raquo;</a></div>
</div>
<div id="leap-perspectives-on-u-s-military-synbio" class="noakes-accordion-item paper">
<div><a href="javascript:void(0);" class="noakes-accordion-item-button">Perspectives on U.S. Military Synbio<br /><span>Cameron Keys</span></a></div>
<div class="noakes-accordion-item-content">
<p><strong>Perspectives on U.S. Military Synbio<br /><a href="http://synbioleap.org/community-member/cameron-keys/">Cameron Keys</a></strong></p>
<p>Some years ago I watched Arizona State University professor Daniel Sarewitz deliver an eye-opening <a href="http://www.ustream.tv/recorded/14182123" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.ustream.tv/recorded/14182123', 'lecture']);" target="_blank">lecture</a> titled “How I Learned to Love the Military Industrial Complex,” in which he isolated the Department of Defense as the most potent source of leverage over energy economies worldwide. This leverage is a source of consternation for activists and would-be reformers concerned with climate change and the on-going dysfunctions of regional world order. However, for Dr. Sarewitz this leverage presents an unprecedented opportunity. In short, anyone looking for an Archimedean pivot for global energy futures would do well to consider the DoD’s role in shaping such futures.<a href="#_ftn1" name="_ftnref1"><sup>[1]</sup></a></p>
<p><a href="http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/Perspectives-on-US-Military.pdf" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','download','http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/06/Perspectives-on-US-Military.pdf']);" target="_blank">Download PDF &raquo;</a></div>
</div>
<div id="leap-synbio-infrastructure-platforms" class="noakes-accordion-item paper">
<div><a href="javascript:void(0);" class="noakes-accordion-item-button">Synbio Infrastructure &#038; Platforms<br /><span>Camille Delebecque</span></a></div>
<div class="noakes-accordion-item-content">
<p><strong>Synbio Infrastructure &#038; Platforms<br /><a href="http://synbioleap.org/community-member/camille-delebecque/">Camille Delebecque</a></strong></p>
<p><strong>How can we support pre-competitive activities in biotechnology? What infrastructure is critical to collaborative efforts? What are potential organizational models?</strong></p>
<p>Synthetic Biology (SynBio) is characterized by a bottom-up approach to designing biological systems for specific purposes (Endy, 2005). In 2014, the European Commission defined the field as &#8220;the application of science, technology and engineering to facilitate and accelerate the design, manufacture and modification of genetic materials in living organisms”. It is a young field, but is now a global endeavor with research and development programs in many countries around the world. The ability to rapidly engineer organisms in laboratories by combining molecular biology tools with engineering principles holds “vast potential for the bioeconomy, and could dramatically transform high-impact fields such as agriculture, manufacturing, energy generation, and medicine” (USA National Bioeconomy Blueprint 2012).</p>
<p><a href="http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Synbio-Infrastructure-_-Platforms.pdf" onclick="_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','download','http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/Synbio-Infrastructure-_-Platforms.pdf']);" target="_blank">Download PDF &raquo;</a></div>
