        <h2>About the conference</h2>
        <div class="text">
          <p>The DNA damage response (DDR) is a complex signalling network including cell cycle checkpoints, DNA repair and damage tolerance pathways. Failure of the DDR or associated events causes genomic instability, an underlining cause of several human syndromes and also associated with various age-related diseases, particularly cancer. Notably, the DDR is also an attractive
            therapeutic target since cytotoxic chemotherapy and radiotherapy are
            widely used as anti-cancer agents and novel DDR-targeting drugs are
          being developed. This EMBO Conference will cover topics including DNA damage detection and signalling, DNA repair, replication, chromatin, ubiquitylation and related processes, ageing, disease and therapeutic applications. </p>
