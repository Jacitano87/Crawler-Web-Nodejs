                <h1><a href="http://haynes.lab.asu.edu/pis-blog/cshl-synthetic-biology-course-2015/">CSHL Synthetic Biology Course &#8211; 2015</a> </h1>
                                <div class="recover"></div>
            </div>
			
			<div class="entry">
				<p>The 2015 Cold Spring Harbor Summer Course in Synthetic Biology will take place July 27 &#8211; August 10.The course will focus on how the complexity of biological systems, combined with traditional engineering approaches, results in the emergence of new design principles for synthetic biology. The Course centers around an <strong>immersive laboratory experience</strong>. Here, students will work in teams to learn the practical and theoretical underpinnings of cutting edge research in the area of Synthetic Biology. Broadly, we will explore how cellular regulation – transcriptional, translational, post-translational, and epigenetic – can be used to engineer cells to accomplish well-defined goals.</p>
<p><strong>Instructors for 2015:</strong><br /><a href="http://dueberlab.berkeley.edu/" target="_blank">John Dueber,</a>University of California, Berkeley<a href="http://www.uvm.edu/%7Ecems/?Page=employee/profile.php&amp;SM=employee/_employeemenu.html&amp;EmID=1058" target="_blank"><br /></a><a href="http://www.cems.uvm.edu/~mjdunlop/" target="_blank">Mary Dunlop</a>, University of Vermont<br /><a href="http://haynes.lab.asu.edu/" target="_blank">Karmella Haynes</a>, Arizona State University<br /><a href="http://www.cheme.cornell.edu/people/profile.cfm?netid=jl564" target="_blank">Julius Lucks</a>, Cornell University<br /><a href="http://www.chemistry.gatech.edu/people/Peralta-Yahya/Pamela%20" target="_blank">Pamela Peralta-Yahya</a>, Georgia Institute of Technology<br />Stanley Qi, Stanford University </p>
<p>Read Instructor bios at  <a href="https://cshlsynbio.wordpress.com/instructors2015/" target="_blank">https://cshlsynbio.wordpress.com/instructors2015/</a></p>
<p><strong>Invited Speakers </strong>are current leaders in synthetic biology, who will present seminars on their research, industrial developments, and other recent developments in the field. Students will have the opportunity to interact with the speakers in an intimate setting:<br /><strong>Elisa Franco</strong>, University of California, Riverside<br /><strong>Emma Frow</strong>, Arizona State University<br /><strong>Mo Khalil</strong>, Boston University<br /><strong>Nathan Hilton</strong>, Joint BioEnergy Institute<br /><strong>Thomas Knight</strong>, Ginkgo BioWorks<br /><strong>Vincent Noireaux</strong>, University of Minnesota<br /><strong>Pamela Silver</strong>, Harvard Medical School<br /><strong>Danielle Tullman-Erceck</strong>, University of California, Berkeley</p>
<p>An up-to-date Speakers list is available at <a href="https://cshlsynbio.wordpress.com/speakers/" target="_blank">https://cshlsynbio.wordpress.com/speakers/</a></p>
<p>For more information about the course and instruction on how to apply, visit <a href="http://cshlsynbio.wordpress.com" target="_blank">http://cshlsynbio.wordpress.com</a>.</p>
