	<section id="profile_bio" class="tab tab--default profile__bio large-8 medium-12 small-12 columns">
	
		<article class="profile__emphasis">
			<h2>Research Emphasis</h2>
			<hr>
			<p>
				Our work is performed in interrelated areas using theoretical, computational, and experimental methods. The theme that unites our studies is the desire to understand and exploit the evolutionary and synthetic design principles of cells. The interrelated areas <strong>comparative functional genomics</strong> to discover and understand the key subsystems in cells and their dynamic organization that allow them to survive and transform the environment and to adapt and evolve to new circumstances; <strong>systems biology</strong> to understand the biophysical, dynamical, and control/information theoretical aspects of cellular network operation in uncertain environments and when modified for applications; and <strong>synthetic biology</strong> for the efficient and predictable engineering of new function in cells for applications of benefit to society.

Ongoing projects in the laboratory fall into the following focus areas:
<ul>
	<li>Systems biology of HIV and HIV Therapies</li>
	<li>Noise, Memory, and Communications in<em> B. subtilis</em> Development and Fitness</li>
	<li>Rapid Characterization and Evidence-Based Annotation of Microbes</li>
	<li>Discovery and Engineering of Tolerance Systems in Biofuel-Related Microbes</li>
	<li>Foundational Technologies for Synthetic Biology</li>
	<li>Foundational Technologies for Systems Biology</li>
	<li>Reverse-Engineering of Cellular Networks</li>
	<li>Molecular Determinants of Microbial Community Activity, Stability, and Energetics</li>
</ul>
<h4></h4>
			</p>
		</article>
	
				<article id="scientist__additional_information" class="profile__additional_information">
			<h2>Additional Information</h2>
			<hr>
			<p>
				The Arkin Laboratory seeks to uncover the evolutionary design principles of cellular networks and populations and exploit them for applications. To do so, researchers are developing a framework to effectively combine comparative functional genomics, quantitative measurement of cellular dynamics, biophysical modeling of cellular networks, and cellular circuit design to ultimately facilitate applications in health, the environment, and bioenergy.
			</p>
		</article>
