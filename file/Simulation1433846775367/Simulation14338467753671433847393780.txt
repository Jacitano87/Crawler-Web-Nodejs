                    <h1 class="title">Articles by Jeff Brock</h1>
                </div>
                
                <div class="content">
                                            <p>Jeff Brock, Manager of Web &amp; Design Services for the <a href="http://biobricks.org">BioBricks Foundation</a>, is a charismatic, right-and-left-brained communicator with more than 14 years expertise in web content, programming, design and usability, and 9 years as a photographer, journalist and media relations professional. Brock uses his expertise in all things digital to ensure that BioBricks Foundation staff, board, and the larger BBF community stay connected, overseeing all the organization’s websites, social-networking accounts, blogs, hosting services, and print and collateral materials.</p>
<p>As owner of the design &amp; development shop <a href="http://www.jeffbrockstudio.com">Jeff Brock Studio</a>, Brock is a staunch proponent of open source software, including WordPress, PHP, and MySQL.</p>

                <h2 class="title entry-title" id="post-305">
                    <a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/2012/05/14/contribute-synbio-community/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent link to Contribute for a strong synbio community">
                        Contribute for a strong synbio community                    </a>
                </h2>
                
                <p class="metadata"><span class="date updated">May 14, 2012</span> in <span class="categories"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/category/uncategorized/" title="View all posts in Uncategorized" rel="category tag">Uncategorized</a></span> by <span class="vcard author"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/author/jeffbrockstudio/" title="Articles by Jeff Brock" class="url fn">Jeff Brock</a></span> | <a class="comments-link" href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/2012/05/14/contribute-synbio-community/#respond">No comments</a></p>            </div>
            
            <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
                                <p>The <a href="http://biobricks.org">BioBricks Foundation</a> (BBF) is a public-benefit organization advancing synthetic biology to benefit all people and the planet. <strong>We have an impact because of people like you</strong> — you are one of a community of thousands of scientists, educators, policymakers, students, and citizens who believe <strong>fundamental biotechnology belongs to all of us</strong>. Thank you for your past support of the BBF. <strong>We’re calling on you now to help the BBF reach important goals in 2012.</strong></p>
<p><strong><a href="https://npo.networkforgood.org/Donate/Donate.aspx?npoSubscriptionId=5282"><img style="border: none; margin-left: 12px;" src="https://d2q0qd5iz04n9u.cloudfront.net/_ssl/proxy.php/http/gallery.mailchimp.com/b703a65bcfd5282409f58de2e/files/button_donate_now.png" alt="Donate Now" width="208" height="54" align="right" /></a>The BBF has big plans for 2012. </strong>We have started organizing the <a href="http://sb6.biobricks.org/"><strong>BioBricks Foundation SB6.0 Conference</strong></a> taking place at Imperial College in London in 2013. We are writing and will distribute free user manuals for the <a href="https://biobricks.org/bpa/"><strong>BioBrick™ Public Agreement</strong></a>, the <a href="http://biobricks.org/programs/education-program/"><strong>Technical RFC Process</strong></a>, and <a href="http://openwetware.org"><strong>OpenWetWare</strong></a>, three powerful tools the BBF created to make your work as a scientist easier. We are also expanding our <a href="http://biobricks.org/programs/education-program/"><strong>Education Program</strong></a> through a partnership with The BioBuilder Educational Foundation to  reach thousands of middle and high-school students who will be the  future leaders of the field. And we are preparing to launch the <a href="http://biobricks.org/programs/technical-program/"><strong>Stanford BIOFAB</strong></a>, a collaborative research effort the BBF helped bring together and whose products will be contributed to the public domain.</p>
<p><strong><a href="https://npo.networkforgood.org/Donate/Donate.aspx?npoSubscriptionId=5282"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-307" title="Donate Now: Help us reach $50K by June 15, 2012" src="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/wp-content/uploads/2012/05/header-spring-email-campaign.png" alt="Donate Now: Help us reach $50K by June 15, 2012" width="300" height="200" /></a>No other nonprofit organization is doing this kind of vital work in this space. </strong>At the BBF, we want to do even more. But we can’t do it without you.</p>
<p><strong>Our annual goal for individual gifts is $100,000. </strong>So far this year, we have raised $37,000, <strong>leaving $63,000 to go</strong>.  For those of you who contributed to our fall 2011 campaign, thank you  so much! If you have not yet contributed, there is still time to do so.  Can you help take us over the $50,000 mark by June 15?</p>
<p><strong>Your gift of $10, $20, $50, $100, or any amount will have a big impact. </strong>The time to give is now.</p>
<p><a href="https://npo.networkforgood.org/Donate/Donate.aspx?npoSubscriptionId=5282"><strong>Make a secure, online donation to the BBF today by clicking this link.</strong></a></p>
<p><strong>If you think a strong synbio community makes the world a better place, please contribute to the BioBricks Foundation today! </strong>We’ll share updates in the coming weeks. Thank you so much for your support!</p>
<p>Sincerely,</p>
<p><img src="http://biobricks.org/wp-content/blogs.dir/1/files/signature-holly-million.png" alt="Holly Million signature" width="182" height="36" /><br />
Holly Million<br />
Executive Director, the BioBricks Foundation</p>

                <h2 class="title entry-title" id="post-300">
                    <a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/2012/02/28/biobricks-foundation-synthetic-biology-6-conference-announced/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent link to BioBricks Foundation Synthetic Biology 6.0 Conference announced: July 9-11, 2013">
                        BioBricks Foundation Synthetic Biology 6.0 Conference announced: July 9-11, 2013                    </a>
                </h2>
                
                <p class="metadata"><span class="date updated">February 28, 2012</span> in <span class="categories"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/category/uncategorized/" title="View all posts in Uncategorized" rel="category tag">Uncategorized</a></span> by <span class="vcard author"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/author/jeffbrockstudio/" title="Articles by Jeff Brock" class="url fn">Jeff Brock</a></span> | <a class="comments-link" href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/2012/02/28/biobricks-foundation-synthetic-biology-6-conference-announced/#respond">No comments</a></p>            </div>
            
            <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
                                <p>Dear SynBio Community Members:</p>
<p>The BioBricks Foundation is pleased to announce <a href="http://sb6.biobricks.org">The BioBricks Foundation Synthetic Biology 6.0 Conference</a> (SB6.0), which will take place on July 9-11, 2013 at Imperial College, London, UK.</p>
<p><a href="http://sb6.biobricks.org" target="_blank"><img style="width: 285px; height: 212px; margin: 0px; float: right; border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" src="https://d2q0qd5iz04n9u.cloudfront.net/_ssl/proxy.php/http/gallery.mailchimp.com/b703a65bcfd5282409f58de2e/files/imperial_college.jpg" alt="Imperial College, London" width="285" height="212" align="right" /></a>This meeting will be the first time in two years that an open and self-defined global community will share, consider, debate, and plan efforts to understand life via building, to make biology easier to engineer, and work together so that the results of such efforts benefit all people and the planet.</p>
<p>SB5.0 was held at Stanford University in June 2011, with the participation of over 70 international speakers and 730 attendees from over 25 countries.</p>
<p>In July 2013, the world comes together once more to learn about and help define what is happening in the world of synthetic biology.</p>
<p>Please join us. For more information regarding the conference, please click on this link: <a href="http://sb6.biobricks.org/">http://sb6.biobricks.org</a></p>
<p>Registration for the conference will open later in 2012. We will let you know when registration officially opens.</p>
<p>Corporate sponsorship and exhibitor opportunities are now available. Please contact <a href="mailto:sponsorship@biobricks.org">sponsorship@biobricks.org</a> for more information.</p>
<p>We will send regular updates throughout the coming months, but please let us know if you have any questions along the way.</p>
<p>Hope to see you at Imperial College, London, UK in July 2013!</p>
<p>Sincerely,</p>
<p>Thi Thach<br />
Communications and Development Manager,<br />
The BioBricks Foundation<a href="mailto:thi@biobricks.org"><br />
thi@biobricks.org</a></p>

                        Winners of BioBricks/OpenWetWare Improvement Survey                    </a>
                </h2>
                
                <p class="metadata"><span class="date updated">January 12, 2012</span> in <span class="categories"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/category/uncategorized/" title="View all posts in Uncategorized" rel="category tag">Uncategorized</a></span> by <span class="vcard author"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/author/jeffbrockstudio/" title="Articles by Jeff Brock" class="url fn">Jeff Brock</a></span> | <a class="comments-link" href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/2012/01/12/winners-improvement-survey/#respond">No comments</a></p>            </div>
            
            <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
                                <div style="text-align: left; margin-bottom: 1em;"><a style="color: #268795;text-decoration: underline;font-weight: normal;" href="http://openwetware.org"><img id="headerImage campaign-icon" style="margin: 0pt; padding: 0pt; display: block; border: 0pt none; height: auto; line-height: 100%; outline: medium none; text-decoration: none;" src="http://gallery.mailchimp.com/b703a65bcfd5282409f58de2e/images/image_13214773460581321477342.jpg" border="0" alt="OpenWetWare and BioBricks Foundation logo" width="466" height="165" /></a></div>
<p>Dear OpenWetWare Community,</p>
<p>Thank you for your participation in the OWW improvement survey. We thank you for all your valuable input toward making <a href="http://openwetware.org" target="_blank">OpenWetWare</a> one of the most effective tools to facilitate your research.</p>
<p>We are thrilled to announce the winners of the OWW survey:</p>
<ul>
<li> Two $25 Amazon gift cards, at random, to two participants who completed the survey questions:
<ol>
<li> <strong><em>Marko Dolinar,</em> University of Ljubljana, Slovenia</strong>.</li>
<li> <strong><em>Leanna Morinishi,</em> MIT, USA</strong></li>
</ol>
</li>
<li> One $50 Amazon gift card, to a participant who gave the best original suggestion(s) for OWW based on usefulness and feasibility:
<ol>
<li> <strong><em>Lee Farrand, </em> Seoul National University, Seoul, Korea</strong></li>
</ol>
</li>
</ul>
<p>We will contact you individually to arrange the delivery of the prizes. Congratulations to the three of you!</p>
<p>It has been a challenging process to choose the winner for the top prize due to the high number of great comments we received. Among these valuable suggestions, Lee Farrand suggested that “On the discussion pages, authors could have &#8216;reputation points,&#8217; allowing us to see who is rated by the community as being a reliable source of information.” Such an approach could contribute to a system for scoring/ranking protocols and other sources of information, a feature that many of you have requested. OWW contributors could increase their reputation points in many ways, including by other users voting their comments as being valuable.</p>
<p>Once again, thank you so much for your participation and valuable input. We wish you a productive year filled with new ideas and discoveries.</p>
<p>Sincerely,</p>
<p>Thi Thach<br />
Communications and Development Manager<br />
The BioBricks Foundation<br />
<a href="mailto:thi@biobricks.org">thi@biobricks.org </a></p>

                <h2 class="title entry-title" id="post-284">
                    <a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/2011/12/28/donate-biobricks-foundation-openwetware/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent link to Donate to BioBricks Foundation and OpenWetWare">
                        Donate to BioBricks Foundation and OpenWetWare                    </a>
                </h2>
                
                <p class="metadata"><span class="date updated">December 28, 2011</span> in <span class="categories"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/category/uncategorized/" title="View all posts in Uncategorized" rel="category tag">Uncategorized</a></span> by <span class="vcard author"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/author/jeffbrockstudio/" title="Articles by Jeff Brock" class="url fn">Jeff Brock</a></span> | <a class="comments-link" href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/2011/12/28/donate-biobricks-foundation-openwetware/#respond">No comments</a></p>            </div>
            
            <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
                                <p><a style="border: none;" href="https://npo.networkforgood.org/Donate/Donate.aspx?npoSubscriptionId=5282"><img style="border: none;" src="http://gallery.mailchimp.com/b703a65bcfd5282409f58de2e/files/header_facebook_v4.jpg" border="0" alt="" /></a></p>
<p><strong>Dear Friends of the BioBricks Foundation,</strong></p>
<p><img class="alignleft" src="https://d2q0qd5iz04n9u.cloudfront.net/_ssl/proxy.php/http/gallery.mailchimp.com/b703a65bcfd5282409f58de2e/files/mailchimp_campaign_poster.jpg" alt="The BioBricks Foundation provides opportunities for SynBio researchers to learn and collaborate." width="341" height="304" align="right" /></p>
<p>Here’s the latest news from the <a href="http://biobricks.org">BioBricks Foundation</a> and our fundraising campaign to raise $50K by December 31, 2011. As of today, <strong>we have already raised $25K toward our goal</strong>. So that leaves just $25K to go! Already made a gift? Thank you so much! <strong>Haven’t given yet? There’s still time to do so.</strong> <a href="https://npo.networkforgood.org/Donate/Donate.aspx?npoSubscriptionId=5282" target="_blank">Help us make it to the top!</a></p>
<p>The BioBricks Foundation (BBF) is a public-benefit organization dedicated to advancing synthetic biology (SynBio) to benefit all people and the planet.                         Support BioBricks &#038; OpenWetWare by Dec. 31                    </a>
                </h2>
                
                <p class="metadata"><span class="date updated">November 29, 2011</span> in <span class="categories"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/category/uncategorized/" title="View all posts in Uncategorized" rel="category tag">Uncategorized</a></span> by <span class="vcard author"><a href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/author/jeffbrockstudio/" title="Articles by Jeff Brock" class="url fn">Jeff Brock</a></span> | <a class="comments-link" href="http://blog.openwetware.org/community/2011/11/29/support-biobricks-openwetware-by-dec-31/#respond">No comments</a></p>            </div>
            
            <div class="content entry-content clearfix">
                                <p><a href="https://npo.networkforgood.org/Donate/Donate.aspx?npoSubscriptionId=5282" style="border: none;"><img src="http://gallery.mailchimp.com/b703a65bcfd5282409f58de2e/files/header_facebook_v4.jpg" border="0" style="border: none;"/></a></p>
<p><strong>Dear Friends of the BioBricks Foundation,</strong></p>
<p>The <a href="http://biobricks.org">BioBricks Foundation</a> (BBF) is a public-benefit organization dedicated to advancing synthetic biology (SynBio) to benefit all people and the planet. We work to create a sustainable future by standardizing biological parts that are safe, ethical, cost effective and publicly accessible. <strong>Your enthusiastic involvement in the SynBio community and the work of the BBF makes a crucial difference.</strong></p>
<p><img style="width: 280px; height: 230px; float: left; border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" src="https://d2q0qd5iz04n9u.cloudfront.net/_ssl/proxy.php/http/gallery.mailchimp.com/b703a65bcfd5282409f58de2e/files/campaign_email_posters_v2.jpg" alt="" width="280" height="230" align="left" /><strong>The BBF is a young organization that has produced results.</strong> This year, we successfully created the first-in-the-world <a title="The BioBrick™ Public Agreement (BPA)" href="http://biobricks.org/bpa/">BioBrick™ Public Agreement</a> (BPA), a legal tool that enables the free exchange and use of standard biological parts. <a title="BIOFAB" href="http://biobricks.org/programs/biofab/">BIOFAB</a>, our first professional research collaboration, has produced reliable, standardized biological parts that constitute a free operating system for biotechnology. In addition, the BBF also manages <a href="http://openwetware.org">OpenWetWare</a> (OWW), an open-source wiki that facilitates teaching, learning, and sharing of data for 9,000 researchers through a culture of collaboration and open science. That’s not all. Just this past June, the BBF successfully organized <a title="SB5.0 Conference Website" href="http://sb5.biobricks.org">SB 5.0</a>, an inspiring 3-day global conference in SynBio with the participation of over 70 international speakers and 730 attendees from over 25 countries.</p>
<p>With 2012 approaching, the BBF plans to do even more to support and connect our SynBio community, promoting the effectiveness of OWW, creating new educational programs, and much more. <strong>We need your help to make these goals a reality.</strong></p>
<p><a href="https://npo.networkforgood.org/Donate/Donate.aspx?npoSubscriptionId=5282"><img border="0" style="width: 208px; height: 54px; margin-left: 24px; border: none;" src="https://d2q0qd5iz04n9u.cloudfront.net/_ssl/proxy.php/http/gallery.mailchimp.com/b703a65bcfd5282409f58de2e/files/button_donate_now.png" alt="Donate Now" width="208" height="54" align="right" /></a>To support this exciting work, <strong>the BBF has launched a fundraising campaign to raise $50K by December 31, 2011</strong>. Thanks to a generous donor, we’ve already raised $8K toward our goal. That leaves just $42K to go!</p>
<p><strong>Your gift of $10, $20, $50, $100, or any amount will have a big impact.</strong> With your help, the BBF will have resources to conduct such activities as launching OWW2.0, providing scholarships to young researchers for SB 6.0, hosting workshops on exciting themes, and launching initiatives to unite global BIOFABs and create a road map for the field of SynBio. The time to give is now. <strong><a href="https://npo.networkforgood.org/Donate/Donate.aspx?npoSubscriptionId=5282">Make a secure, online donation to the BBF today.</a></strong></p>
<p><strong><img style="width: 280px; height: 217px; float: right; border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" src="https://d2q0qd5iz04n9u.cloudfront.net/_ssl/proxy.php/http/gallery.mailchimp.com/b703a65bcfd5282409f58de2e/files/campaign_email_crowd.1.jpg" alt="" width="280" height="217" align="right" /></strong>We live in a world with limited natural resources. Striving to build a sustainable world is no longer a luxury, but a necessity. SynBio may hold the key to our future. The BBF fills a unique need in this field, educating, building community, creating legal tools, and fostering cooperation. <strong>Please act now by <a href="https://npo.networkforgood.org/Donate/Donate.aspx?npoSubscriptionId=5282">making a personal financial gift</a> to support the BBF.</strong></p>
<p>We’ll let you know our progress in meeting our campaign goal. <strong>Thank you so much for your support.</strong></p>
<p>Sincerely,</p>
