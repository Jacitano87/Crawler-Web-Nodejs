	<section id="profile_bio" class="tab tab--default profile__bio large-8 medium-12 small-12 columns">
	
		<article class="profile__emphasis">
			<h2>Research Emphasis</h2>
			<hr>
			<p>
				<a href="http://pbd.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Mutalik.png"><img class="alignright wp-image-49170" src="http://pbd.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/Mutalik.png" alt="Mutalik" width="531" height="398" /></a>My past work has been focused on how to predictably engineer gene expression in a microbe and how quantitative characterization, modeling and biomolecular design can vastly increase the reliability of engineered circuits in bacteria. We have created a suite of synthetic biology tools for rational engineering of gene expression in <em>E. coli</em>, developed predictive DNA sequence-activity relationships that yield insight into important biological mechanisms, devised methods to score genetic parts by quantifying the context effects on parts performance, and uncovered design principles in microbial stress responses. These contributions are at the core of what distinguishes the emerging field of synthetic biology from the founder fields of genetic and metabolic engineeringâ€”a focus on making genetic parts and circuit design rational, predictable and reliable. All of these are necessary steps towards realizing the real potential for biological solutions to current problems in medicine, energy and environment.
<div></div>
My current research interests are focused on these following themes:
<ol>
	<li>Developing functional genomics and discovery platform for thousands of microbial genomes.</li>
	<li>Engineering a universal synthetic biology platform for reliable expression of heterologous genes in <em>E. coli</em>. Study regulatory design principles in microbial stress responses and use so learnt lessons to engineer robust chassis for biotechnology applications.</li>
	<li>Systematically develop a large panel of synthetic genetic parts for phylogenetically distinct microbes and define rules for their functional composition; to develop sequence-activity relationships for variety of regulatory genetic parts such as promoters (Transcriptional units), ribosome binding sites (translation units), mRNA stabilizers, attenuators, antisense RNAs, riboswitches, transcription factors, etc.</li>
	<li>To develop a standardized approach in designing and optimizing the performance of multi-gene systems by using reliable genetic parts and applying statistical design of experiments.</li>
</ol>
&nbsp;
			</p>
		</article>
