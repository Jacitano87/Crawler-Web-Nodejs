	<section id="profile_bio" class="tab tab--default profile__bio large-8 medium-12 small-12 columns">
	
		<article class="profile__emphasis">
			<h2>Research Emphasis</h2>
			<hr>
			<p>
				<h3>Computational Methods Development<a href="http://pbd.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/density_crop.gif"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-16555" src="http://pbd.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/density_crop.gif" alt="density_crop" width="121" height="121" /></a></h3>
Much of my research is focused on the development of new algorithms and methods for addressing problems in structural biology. In collaboration with researchers from Los Alamos National Lab, Duke University and Cambridge University we continue the development of <a href="http://www.phenix-online.org/" target="_blank">PHENIX</a>, a software system for the automated solution of macromolecular structures using crystallographic methods. We are also pursuing projects that expand the scope of PHENIX to include the analysis of neutron diffraction data, and single particle cryo-EM reconstruction methods.
<h3></h3>
<h3>Bioenergy Research and Structural Biology<a href="http://pbd.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/miscanthus.jpg"><img class="alignright wp-image-16557 size-full" src="http://pbd.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/miscanthus.jpg" alt="" width="120" height="120" /></a></h3>
Global warming has become a major world issue and carbon dioxide gas emissions from the burning of fossil fuels are a significant contributor to the problem. The need to develop carbon-neutral and renewable sources of energy has become a priority. The conversion of cellulosic (plant) material to fuels such as ethanol has the potential to provide a significant fraction of fuel in the future. In the <a title="JBEI Technology Division" href="http://www.jbei.org/research/divisions/technology/" target="_blank">Technology Division</a> of JBEI, we are developing and employing new technologies to improve the conversion of biomass to fuels, including methods<span style="color: #000000;"> for the characterization of genes and proteins in both natural and engineered plants and microorganisms, synthetic biology approaches for engineering new fuel-generating organisms and improved fuel plants, and <a href="http://pbd.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Data-Technology-Image_3MMW.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-48843" src="http://pbd.wpengine.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Data-Technology-Image_3MMW-300x184.jpg" alt="Data-Technology-Image_3MMW" width="300" height="184" /></a>imaging techniques for more detailed visualization of plant cell walls and their components. The <a title="JBEI Structural Biology" href="http://www.jbei.org/research/divisions/technology/structural-biology/">Structural Biology Group </a>of the JBEI Technology Division uses high resolution X-ray crystallography to understand the atomic structure of proteins involved in biofuels production. </span>
			</p>
		</article>
