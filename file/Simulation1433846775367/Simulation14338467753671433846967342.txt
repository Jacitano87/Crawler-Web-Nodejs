						<h1>The LEAP Organizers</h1>
<h3></h3>
<h3>The LEAP team is the foundation that anchors LEAP and its activities. They engage, coordinate and grow the LEAP community and vision.</h3>
<div class="noakes-accordion community-member" rel="leap" data-keep-centered="true" data-include-close-button="true" data-include-arrow="true">
<div id="leap-megan-palmer" class="noakes-accordion-item community-member">
<div><a href="javascript:void(0);" class="noakes-accordion-item-button"><img width="200" height="200" src="http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/07/megan-square-200x200.png" class="thumbnail wp-post-image" alt="megan square" /><br />Megan Palmer</a></div>
<div class="noakes-accordion-item-content">
<h3>Executive Director</h3>
<p>Megan Palmer’s work focuses on the responsible development of synthetic biology and biotechnology more broadly. She is the William J. Perry fellow in International Security at the Stanford Center for International Security and Cooperation (CISAC) and a Research Fellow at the California Institute for Quantitative Biosciences (QB3) at the University of California Berkeley. Concurrently, she is an investigator and serves as Deputy Director of Policy &amp; Practices for the multi-university NSF Synthetic Biology Engineering Research Center (Synberc). Megan previously was a postdoctoral scholar in the Department of Bioengineering at Stanford. She received a PhD from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in Biological Engineering in 2010, and a B.Sc.E. in Engineering Chemistry from Queen&#8217;s University in Ontario, Canada in 2004.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
</div>
</div>
<div id="leap-vrunda-rathod" class="noakes-accordion-item community-member">
<div><a href="javascript:void(0);" class="noakes-accordion-item-button"><img width="200" height="200" src="http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/vrunda-square1.png" class="thumbnail wp-post-image" alt="vrunda square" /><br />Vrunda Rathod</a></div>
<div class="noakes-accordion-item-content">
<h3>Program Director</h3>
<p>Vrunda Rathod’s work focuses on science and technology policy in the US. She holds an undergraduate degree from USC in Biomedical Engineering and a graduate degree in Biomedical Science Policy from Georgetown University. She began her career in the policy world working on the policy agenda at the American Institute for Medical and Biological Engineering, dealing with a diverse range of issues from patents to bioethics. Vrunda then went to work for US Congresswoman Diane Watson as a legislative aide focusing on both national and global health care, foreign affairs, and environmental policy. When the Congresswoman retired, she decided to move back to California and spent time consulting for Kaiser Permanente, until an exciting opportunity in Liberia came along. She worked with the Clinton Health Access Initiative to research maternal and newborn deaths in the country. She is now back in California, working with SynBio LEAP, helping create a leadership program for synthetic biologists.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
</div>
</div>
<div id="leap-rachel-lawely" class="noakes-accordion-item community-member">
<div><a href="javascript:void(0);" class="noakes-accordion-item-button"><img width="200" height="200" src="http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/130108_Rachel_cropped-200x200.jpg" class="thumbnail wp-post-image" alt="130108_Rachel_cropped" /><br />Rachel Lawley</a></div>
<div class="noakes-accordion-item-content">
<h3>Community Director</h3>
<p>Rachel has spent her career supporting the development and growth of organizations looking to change the world.  Most recently, through her work at FURTHER by Design, she has focused on creating ‘systems change’ through collaborative innovation.  She has been instrumental in the development of tools and programs to facilitate collaboration across sectors, and she brings this systems perspective to the organizations she supports.</p>
<p>Prior to FURTHER, Rachel served as Vice Consul for Trade &amp; Investment for the British Consulate in San Francisco, leading healthcare/life sciences economic development initiatives for the British Government in Northern California, Montana, Wyoming, Alaska and Idaho. Rachel started her career with Origo, a social enterprise consultancy, and has spent over a decade supporting non-profit, for-profit and social enterprise organizations with business development and new market strategies, as well as creative approaches to organizational structure and partnerships.  She received her B.S. in Psychology from Lancaster University in the United Kingdom.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
</div>
</div>
<div id="leap-hamaoui-jeff" class="noakes-accordion-item community-member">
<div><a href="javascript:void(0);" class="noakes-accordion-item-button"><img width="200" height="200" src="http://synbioleap.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/09/Jeff-Hamaoui-photo-200x200.jpg" class="thumbnail wp-post-image" alt="Jeff Hamaoui photo" /><br />Jeff Hamaoui</a></div>
<div class="noakes-accordion-item-content">
<h3>Special Projects</h3>
<p>Jeff Hamaoui is an expert in collaborative innovation and a master facilitator.  Fascinated with open innovation, systems collaborations and the intersection between the two, Jeff has spent twenty years building partnerships around the world.  He has worked with fortune 500 companies, governments, investors and NGOs trying to understand the strategies, technologies, financial tools and narratives to effectively get groups of people to design together, invest together and work together.  Jeff was co-founder of LAUNCH, a global sustainability innovations program supported by NASA, USAID, Department of State and NIKE, and sits on the board of TechSoup Global, which focuses on technology for social change.  Jeff received his MA in Education from London University (1993) and BA in Philosophy and Literature from Swansea University (1992). He is fluent in French and Spanish.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
</div>
