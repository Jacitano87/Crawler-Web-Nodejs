				<div class="page-content">
						<h2><strong>Postdocs</strong></h2>
<p><strong></strong>Researchers interested in a postdoctoral position are welcome to send a query and a C.V. Please consider applying for the following fellowship(s).</p>
<p><strong>Bisgrove Postdoctoral Program</strong>: Each award is for $100,000 per year for two years. Deadline to Apply is <strong>Friday, January 9, 2015</strong>. For eligibility and application details, visit <a title="Science Foundation AZ Grant Opportunities" href="http://www.sfaz.org/grant-opportunities/" target="_blank">http://www.sfaz.org/grant-opportunities/</a>. The application packet is available as a pdf download here: <a title="Bisgrove Application Packet" href="http://www.sfaz.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/2015-Bisgrove-RFP.pdf" target="_blank">http://www.sfaz.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/2015-Bisgrove-RFP.pdf</a></p>
<h2>Graduate Students</h2>
<p><strong>PhD students</strong>: The Haynes lab is seeking one graduate student for Fall 2014 &#8211; Spring 2015 for a project aimed at using synthetic proteins to epigenetically enhance synthetic gene expression in cultured human cells. The student must qualify for an <a href="http://more.engineering.asu.edu/grad/graduate-fellowships/deans-fellowship/" target="_blank">ASU FSE Dean&#8217;s Fellowship</a>. Although the applicant may be from any of the Fulton Schools of Engineering relevant to cell and tissue engineering, prospective students are encouraged to apply to the <a href="http://engineering.asu.edu/graduate/bme" target="_blank">Graduate Programs in Biomedical Engineering</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Masters Students</strong>: We have two spots available each semester for the Masters Applied Project class. Prerequisites include Advanced/Molecular Synthetic Biology (Dr. Haynes), Modeling of Nonlinear Systems (Dr. Wang), or any quantitative molecular biology course.</p>
<h2>Undergraduate Students</h2>
<p><strong>Freshmen</strong>: Please inquire about a <a href="http://openwetware.org/wiki/Haynes:Internal#Undergraduate_Volunteers" target="_blank">volunteer position</a>. </p>
<p><strong>Sophomores, Juniors</strong>: After volunteering in the lab for one semester, you can learn advanced techniques and apply for a <a href="http://engineering.asu.edu/furi" target="_blank">FURI</a> fellowship.</p>
<p><strong>BME Seniors</strong>: if you have completed a FURI project in my lab (in the <span style="text-decoration: underline;">Spring of your Junior year or prior to that</span>), you may inquire about completing a Capstone senior design project in synthetic biology under my mentorship. <br /><em></em></p>
<p><strong>All undergrads</strong>: The International Genetically Engineered Machines (iGEM) competition, which runs every summer, is also a great opportunity to do undergraduate research in synthetic biology. Visit the <a href="http://studentorgs.engineering.asu.edu/igem/" target="_blank">ASU iGEM Team site</a> for more information.</p>
<p><strong>Special Undergrad Paid Positions</strong>:</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Spark Outreach Project kit Designer</span> &#8211; Help to design and build a DNA analysis kit for local Arizona 11th and 12th graders. The position requires lab work for 2 &#8211; 5 hours per week, basic machine assembly skills, basic knowledge of polymerase chain reaction (PCR),  and availability to meet with the Spark coordinator once per week. Demonstration of good protocol/ instruction-authorship and outreach experience are desired. <strong>Target start date</strong>: November 1, 2014. <strong>Application</strong>: Please submit a 1-page letter of intent and a current c.v./ resume to Dr. Karmella Haynes.</p>
<h2>High School Students</h2>
<p>The Haynes lab hosts interns from the ASU Engineering Summer Research Program for High School Students. Please contact Dr. Jeffery Labelle at ASU for information on how to apply. Then, send Dr. Haynes a resume/c.v. and a short explanation of why you want to work on a synthetic biology project in our lab.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://2013hs.igem.org/Main_Page">International Genetically Engineered Machines (iGEM) competition high school division</a>, which runs every spring, is a great opportunity to do synthetic biology at your school. Ask one of your teachers to help organize and lead a team at your school. You can contact Dr. Haynes for advice and information.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Contact the P.I. at karmella.haynes <em>at</em> asu <em>dot</em> edu if you are interested in any of these opportunities.</strong></p>
