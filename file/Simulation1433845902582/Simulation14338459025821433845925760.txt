    <h2><a href="/content/advances-gene-expression-control-active-translational-coupling" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Advances in gene expression control via active translational coupling">Advances in gene expression control via active translational coupling</a></h2>
            <span class="timestamp">11 Jan 2012</span>
      </div>
  <div class="content clear-block">
        	<div class="node-author">Posted by kcosta</div>
            <p>BIOFAB has developed a method that allows for reliable functional coupling of prokaryotic transcription and translation control elements with user-specific coding sequences for genes of interest. The BIOFAB team engineered a collection of standard biological parts that implements the method across a range of transcription and translation levels, and with native and also a phage RNA polymerase. For the genes tested in the system so far, BIOFAB achieved &gt;90% predictability of gene expression to within a two-fold protein concentration of the target window. Team Leader Vivek Mutalik announced the work during Cambridge Healthtech Institute&#39;s Third Annual <a href="http://www.chi-peptalk.com/pxv">Engineering Genes, Vectors, Constructs and Clones Conference</a> on January 9, 2012.</p>

    <h2><a href="/content/dna20-partners-with-biofab" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to DNA2.0 Partners with BIOFAB to Offer Biological Building Blocks Through Gene Designer">DNA2.0 Partners with BIOFAB to Offer Biological Building Blocks Through Gene Designer</a></h2>
            <span class="timestamp">28 Jul 2011</span>
      </div>
  <div class="content clear-block">
        	<div class="node-author">Posted by kcosta</div>
            <p><a href="https://www.dna20.com/" target="_blank">DNA2.0</a> today announced that the first collection of biological building blocks characterized by the BIOFAB International Open Facility Advancing Biotechnology (BIOFAB) are now available for use in the design and assembly of genes within DNA2.0&rsquo;s <a class="inline" href="https://www.dna20.com/genedesigner2/" target="_blank">Gene Designer</a> software. The free-to-use BIOFAB genetic parts are distributed as virtual sequences in the newly minted gene marketplace embedded within DNA2.0&rsquo;s gene design and assembly application. This &ldquo;app store&rdquo; for molecular biology and biotechnology provides scientists and bioengineers access to powerful preassembled and validated DNA elements for making genetic constructs with Gene Designer. DNA2.0 becomes the first commercial vendor to provide access&mdash;through a few clicks of a mouse&mdash;to the initial collection of biological parts developed by BIOFAB.</p>

    <h2><a href="/content/sbol-workshop-participants-argue-full-sequence-disclosure" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to SBOL workshop participants argue for full [sequence] disclosure">SBOL workshop participants argue for full [sequence] disclosure</a></h2>
            <span class="timestamp">13 Jan 2011</span>
      </div>
  <div class="content clear-block">
        	<div class="node-author">Posted by kcosta</div>
            <p>Following a discussion by the workgroup for Data Standards in Synthetic Biology, which met in June 2010 during the <a href="http://cctbio.ece.umn.edu/wiki/index.php/IWBDA:International_Workshop_on_Bio_Design_Automation" target="new">Second Workshop on Biodesign Automation</a>, several participants (including a number of BIOFAB and <a href="http://www.synberc.org">SynBERC</a> investigators) published a <a href="http://www.nature.com/nbt/journal/v29/n1/full/nbt.1753.html" target="new">Nature Biotechnology letter to the editor</a> arguing that articles reporting synthetic gene networks should always disclose the complete sequence of all constructs described. They note that without such information, it is challenging if not impossible to reproduce and build upon others' work, which is a central tenet of the engineering of biology. The full letter, entitled "Essential information for synthetic DNA sequences" and published January 10, 2011, is at <a href="http://www.nature.com/nbt/journal/v29/n1/full/nbt.1753.html" target="new">Nature Biotechnology</a>.</p>

    <h2><a href="/first_datasheet" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to BIOFAB produces its first datasheet">BIOFAB produces its first datasheet</a></h2>
            <span class="timestamp">17 Mar 2010</span>
      </div>
  <div class="content clear-block">
        	<div class="node-author">Posted by kcosta</div>
            <p>The BIOFAB&#39;s first datasheet provides information about three previously uncharacterized promoters that we obtained from the MIT <a href="http://parts.mit.edu">Registry of Standard Biological Parts</a>. We will use such datasheets to formally capture and share information about the parts that we build and characterize (<a href="http://www.nature.com/nbt/journal/v26/n7/full/nbt0708-771.html">see Canton et. al., 2008</a>). We hope that all users, ranging from theoretical biophysicists to highschool students, will help us to develop quality measurements based upon their needs and application. An understanding of quality will help to define specific objectives for the build-out of our operational capacities.</p>
